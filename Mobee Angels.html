01 — Brief

A doll that carries
cultural memory

Mobee Angels makes handcrafted dolls using Ankara fabric — the bold, geometric, wax-print textile associated with West African identity and celebration. The product isn't just a toy; it's an heirloom object that connects children and families to a cultural tradition.

The brief was to build a brand identity that matched the energy of the product: handmade, expressive, culturally rich — without falling into surface-level exoticism. The mark needed to work as a product character and as a logo simultaneously.

"The doll is the brand. The brand is the doll."

02 — Insight

The fabric is the
visual language

Ankara fabric carries pattern, colour, and meaning in every weave. Rather than illustrating a generic doll and applying the fabric as texture, the illustration builds the form from the fabric — the wings, body, and dress ARE the Ankara pattern.

This makes the mark immediately legible as a product promise: every doll is made from this fabric, with this care, carrying this heritage. The angel silhouette adds aspiration — these are gifts, not just toys.

03 — Identity

Mark, wordmark,
and palette

The identity has two layers: the character mark (the doll alone, for product tags and stamps) and the full lockup (doll + wordmark for brand presence). The wordmark replaces the "O" in Mobee with an orange circle — a small typographic gesture that echoes the orange accent woven through the Ankara pattern.

Logo system

Character mark used standalone for swing tags, stamps, and embossed labels. Full lockup — doll + wordmark — used on packaging, signage, and digital. The rounded heavy type echoes the hand-stitched weight of the product.

Mark

Character as logo

The doll illustration functions as the primary brand mark — not a mascot applied on top of a logo, but the logo itself. Scaled down to 16px it holds as a simple silhouette.

Typography

Round, heavy, handmade

Chunky rounded letterforms in the wordmark echo the tactile, stitched quality of the product. The "O" orange-dot treatment ties back to the Ankara accent palette without forcing a graphic element.

Illustration

Ankara fabric in vector

Geometric wax-print patterns translated into flat vector illustration — bold outlines, layered colour, and deliberate texture marks that reference the handmade origin of each doll.

Colour

Pulled from the fabric

The five-colour palette is lifted directly from the Ankara textile — no supplementary brand colours invented. Every colour in the identity exists in the physical product.
