01 — Brief

Building something you can hold.

Brand work is flat — surface, colour, mark. This was the opposite exercise: a smartwatch concept, modeled from scratch in Blender, to learn how a form actually holds together in three dimensions. The strap, the bezel, the sensor housing, the way light catches a speckled plastic finish — none of that exists until you've actually built it.

CS-500 isn't a client brief or a product going to market. It's a study — a deliberately complex hard-surface object chosen because wearables force you to solve small-scale mechanical detail (hinges, screws, strap tension) that flatter shapes let you avoid.

The flat work taught me to design the surface. This taught me to design what's underneath it.

02 — Process

Four angles,
one story

A model that only looks right from one camera isn't finished — it's a good screenshot. Every angle in this set had to justify itself: the fisheye close-up for the face and materials, the straight top-down for proportion, the 3/4 hero shot for the pitch.

Getting a render to hold up from more than one angle is where the modeling mistakes actually show up — topology that reads fine head-on falls apart in profile. That's the real test this study was built to fail at first, and eventually pass.
